Week 5: Product Demos, Value Selling & Objection Handling

Day 1 | Topic: Introduction to Chalk Talks

**Story:** A **Chalk Talk** is a visual, conceptual whiteboard session that frames a problem before you show the product. It‚Äôs a powerful tool to take control of the conversation, establish yourself as a thought leader, and align the customer's pain with your solution's value proposition. This moves the focus from features to business outcomes.

üìò Key Terms

  • **Chalk Talk:** A conceptual, non-product presentation used to align on pain/solution.
  • **Conceptual Selling:** Focusing on the buyer's concepts and problems, not product specifications.
  • **Whiteboarding:** Using diagrams to visually explain complex processes and how your product fits.

üíº Use Case

Before starting the software demo, you draw a high-level diagram of the client's current process and point out the "bottlenecks," establishing a shared understanding of the problem that your solution is explicitly designed to solve.

W5 Focus: Conceptual vs. Feature Time

Day 2 | Topic: Value-First vs. Feature-First Selling

**Story:** Amateurs sell features ("What it does"). Professionals sell **Value** ("Why you should care"). **Value-First Selling** requires that every feature demonstrated is immediately translated into a tangible business benefit‚Äîbe it increased revenue, reduced risk, or saved time. This is key to differentiating yourself from the competition.

üìò Key Terms

  • **Value-First:** Demonstrating the ROI or benefit before detailing the technical feature.
  • **Feature-First:** Describing product functionality without translating it into a customer benefit.
  • **ROI Justification:** Quantifying the return on investment the client will receive.

üíº Use Case

Instead of saying, "This tool integrates with 20 systems," you say, "This integration saves your team 10 hours a week on manual data entry, which is the equivalent of adding a part-time employee to your finance department (**Value-First**)."

Impact of Value-First Selling on Close Rate

Day 5 | Topic: Handling Technical and Non-Technical Objections

**Story:** Every deal will hit an obstacle. You must master the art of addressing **Technical Objections** (e.g., "Will it integrate?") and **Non-Technical Objections** (e.g., "The price is too high"). üìò Key Terms

  • **Non-Technical Objection:** Concerns related to price, timing, or budget.
  • **Isolate & Re-Anchor:** Technique to confirm the true objection before providing the value-based counter-argument.

üíº Use Case

Customer: "We're worried about implementation time" (**Technical Objection**). You respond: "I hear you; if we could deploy in 10 days, would you be ready to move forward?" (Isolate), allowing you to immediately introduce the pre-built API (Re-Anchor).
